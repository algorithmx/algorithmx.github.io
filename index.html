Molecular dynamics simulation of ion implantation.

The ion implantation is a frequently used process in semiconductor device fabrication. Dopants are implanted into the desired regions of a semiconductor wafer to alter the electrical properties. The process is usually simulated by tracing the trajectory of the ion inside the crystalline or amorphous material. Along the trajectory, the energy exchange between the ion and the material is recorded for analysis of damage on the material. The ion stops when it loses all its energy.

The fastest simulation uses the Binary Collision Approximation, where an ion hops from consecutive collision events. In this approximation, the trajectory is approximated by zigzag lines. Various details must be considered in a consistant manner to produce realistic simulation results.

On the other hand, the molecular dynamics simulation is accurate and simple to implement. Click for an animation of 10,000 ions implanted into a simple cubic crystal. In the simulation I disregard the recoil of the atoms in the crystal.

Simulation of the LLG equation on 2d ferromagnet with skyrmions embeded.

I am working on skyrmions over my entire PhD. Here is an animation for a pair of skkrmions in a ferromagnet, which is modelled by a lattice of magnetization vectors and Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert equation with nearest and next-nearest neighbor coupling.

One crucial point in writing the discrete LLG equation is to make sure the magnetization vectors are normalized at each time step. This is common in all dynamical simulations of system with constraints. For magnetization, the constraint is that its amplitude is constant. The naive way of "update and project" will not work. The correct way is to work in a local, intrinsic coordinate system.

Kinetic Monte-Carlo simulation of particle diffusion on 2d honeycomb lattice.

Kinetic Monte-Carlo is a simulation strategy to simulate the dynamics of a system of reactants. Compared to the common method of time discretization, time evolution in KMC is based on events.

The diffusion dynamics can be modelled as a system of reactants. The diffusion process is modelled as a series of events, each of which is a particle diffusion from one site to another. We adapt a Eulerian perspective of the system into which the particles diffuse, so that the states are the site occupations in each unit cell and the reaction rules are determined by the motion of the diffusants.

Click for a 2D illustration of diffusion process with unrealistic diffusant hopping rules.

Simulated annealing optimization of configuration of wires with collision avoidance.

This project is to solve a problem of assembly L-shaped wires on a board. The wires are attached vertically on the board at approximately equidistant contact points. Once attached, a wire can rotate along the x-axis. There are constraints on the smallest distance between any pair of wires. The goal is to find an overall configuration that satisfies the constraints and minimizes the total spreading angle of the wires.

I got this problem from my friend who is working in a factory. His employee usually spend an hour to manipulate a hundred wires to get a good configuration. With my simulation and visualization, he systematically improved the wire attaching workflow.

Click for an animation of the simulated annealing algorithm on this problem. It is a standalone webpage. Hope the javascript code inspires you and help you to build your own simulation.
