Theoretical Foundations

Maxwell's Equations

Foundation of electromagnetic field theory describing all electromagnetic phenomena.

• Gauss's Law

• Faraday's Law

• Ampère's Law

• Gauss's Law for Magnetism

Eigenmode Analysis

Spectral decomposition to identify and separate relevant electromagnetic phenomena.

• Eigenvalue problems

• Spectral theory

• Mode relevance filtering

• Frequency domain analysis

Energy Conservation

Energy-based mapping between electromagnetic fields and circuit parameters.

• Magnetic energy ↔ Inductance

• Electric energy ↔ Capacitance

• Dissipated energy ↔ Resistance

• Energy conservation validation

Graph Theory

Mathematical foundation for circuit topology and connectivity representation.

• Node-edge relationships

• Circuit topology optimization

• Connectivity matrices

• Graph algorithms

Darwin's Model

Quasistatic approximation bridging field theory and circuit theory.

• Second-order approximation

• Same phenomena as circuits

• Theoretical consistency

• Bridge between domains

Validation Framework

Comprehensive validation ensuring model accuracy and physical consistency.

• Energy conservation checks

• Passivity enforcement

• Reciprocity verification

• Convergence analysis

Applications

EMC Analysis

Detection of coupling paths in complex 3D structures for electromagnetic compatibility studies.

Power Electronics

Parasitic effect modeling and efficiency optimization for power electronic components.

Multi-domain Simulation

Integration with thermal and mechanical models for comprehensive system analysis.

Design Optimization

Rapid evaluation of design variants and automated optimization processes.
